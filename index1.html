Why this guide?

We've all been there before. You arrive in a new, huge city, confused and on a tight schedule. You consult lonely planet and wikitravel and come up short - They don't know what I like! This is a guide personally tailored for Zoe Ofer, with all her quirks and inclinations taken into account.

Thanks

This guide would not have passed into being without help from several persons: Noam Modai (technical assistance), Jonathan Blaiberg (tips and pointers) and Eden Elhamias (more tips and pointers!)

Cafes & Bakeries

All of the cups of coffee and croissants you could possibly need in two weeks.

My Little Cup

Charming, pretty cafe.

Kaffabar

A Cafe that greatly respects it's coffee.

Charli Boulangerie

Famous Bakery, excellent for a quick pastry for the road.

Coco Donuts

Small, homey donuts bakery.

Recommended by Eden Elhamias

Karsmakers

Another bakery - they specialize in bagels.

Recommended by Eden Elhamias

Food

The best in brussels culinary.

Bia Mara

Excellent Fish & Chips joint. They also serve Shnitzel

Recommended by Eden Elhamias

Fin de Siècle

A high quality, "Belgian" restaurant. They serve traditional Belgian food. Get here early, as you can't book and the place is popular.

12-20 Euros per main course

Comme Chez Soi

Most renowned restaurant in Brussels. Go here if you want to splash out on excellent food. Order in advance.

30+ Euros per main course

Fritkot

Famous frites joint (belgian chips, some say they are the best).

Cheap and yummy!

Maison Dandoy

Come here for artisanal Belgian waffle.

Brussels waffle = light & fluffy, Liege waffle = chewy & sweet
